What's inside

7 emails. One shift per week.

No fluff. No motivational filler. Each email gives you one idea and one thing to try — small enough to do on a messy day.

1

Day 1

Your brain isn't broken. The system is.

Why forcing neurotypical routines always backfires — and what's actually happening when you "can't follow through". The reframe that changes everything before we touch a single habit.

2

Day 3

The shame spiral — and how to get out of it fast.

How guilt and shame actively block executive function. A simple pattern interrupt you can use within minutes — no journaling, no breathing exercises, no toxic positivity.

3

Day 5

Your energy map — the 15-minute version.

Stop planning your week by the clock. Start planning it by your actual energy. A simple map you can fill in once and use for months — even when your schedule changes completely.

4

Day 8

The minimum timebox — how to start when your brain says no.

Motivation doesn't come before action — it comes after. The minimum timebox is the smallest unit of real work you can commit to. One tool that replaces willpower with a low-friction trigger.

5

Day 11

Decision hygiene — make fewer decisions, finish more things.

Decision fatigue is real and hits neurodivergent brains harder. How to pre-decide the small stuff so your cognitive budget goes to actual work — not negotiating with yourself all day.

6

Day 14

Your superpowers — what you're actually sharp at.

Most neurodivergent people know their struggles in detail. Far fewer have mapped their actual leverage points. A simple framework to identify the conditions where you do your best work — and how to design around them.

7

Day 17

Your first system — small, honest, and yours.

Putting it together: a 3-part minimum system built from what you've learned. Not a productivity framework. Not a template. A starting point you actually own — that works whether your week is predictable or not.
