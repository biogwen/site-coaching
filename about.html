Why this exists

Neurodivergent people are often offered two bad options: force neurotypical routines until burnout, or accept chaos and call it personality.

I do not buy either. Neurodivergent and More exists to build a third option: a structure that holds — built around how your brain actually runs.

We focus on the few changes that create stability: energy management, working from your actual strengths, and execution systems that hold up in messy weeks.

The founder

Built by someone who lives it

I'm Gwenaël. I'm autistic, ADHD, HPE, highly sensitive, and hyper-emotional. A creative chaos — mostly under control.

I spent a few years working like everyone else in IT. Then I discovered Agile coaching, and started asking a different question: how do you help neurodivergent people the way I needed to be helped — without manipulation, without guilt, without pretending to be someone else?

I studied different approaches. I applied what neuroscience and Agile had to offer. Then I kept only what had actually allowed me to live well — without a mask. That became the foundation of everything I teach.

After nearly 15 years of coaching, working with 500+ professionals and organizations including the European Commission, Spectrum, and Thales — and 2 years living and working across 9 countries as a digital nomad — I know one thing: the system doesn't need to fit the norm. It needs to fit you.

"Energy management beats willpower. Strength mapping beats self-improvement. Systems beat discipline. This isn't theory — this is what actually worked for me, and what I help others build."

For people who move

Your system keeps breaking every time you move.
That's not you. That's the system.

Most productivity frameworks assume you have a stable address, a fixed routine, and the same timezone every week. If you're a digital nomad — especially a neurodivergent one — you don't. Every move resets your environment, your sleep, your energy, your habits. And most systems collapse with it.

📦

Every move = system restart

New city, new routine, new overload. You spend the first two weeks just trying to function — and the system you built is already gone.

🔋

Constant adaptation drains energy

New sounds, new spaces, new social cues. For a neurodivergent brain, adapting costs more than for most people. There's nothing left for actual work.

🌍

No community that gets it

Nomad communities talk about freedom and sunsets. They don't talk about executive function crashes in Chiang Mai or sensory overload in a new coworking space.

The solution

Systems that travel with you

→

Fast-reset routines — rebuild your baseline in 2–3 days, not 2–3 weeks

→

Energy management for motion — protect your capacity before, during, and after a move

→

Async-first coaching — sessions scheduled around your timezone, wherever you are

→

A community that gets it — nomads who are also neurodivergent, not just optimizing for sunsets
